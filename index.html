The Band

Big Mama Gayle

Vocals

Big Mama Gayle returns us to an era in show business when entertainment was truly entertaining. Theatrically inspired by lavish showbiz icons such as old-time cabaret and Las Vegas productions, and with enough costumes to make a drag queen feel under-dressed, Big Mama Gayle is 100% a pure entertainer.

Beginning in musical theater during the 1960's, Big Mama Gayle's career took her to the golden stages of Broadway. Gayle found herself in shows on the East Coast, West Coast, abroad, and everywhere in between. Gayle even graced the pages of noteworthy publications such as Life Magazine and - yes, it's true - Playboy!

With countless props and an estimated six costume changes per hour, Big Mama Gayle's rock and blues show flies by with splashes of sequins, flurries of feather boas, and the occasional inflatable monkey. It's glitz, glamour, Rock 'n' Roll, and side-splitting moments for the whole family.

The Sugar Daddies

Not to be overlooked, Gayle's Sugar Daddies are five of the hottest musicians to be found in the Pacific Northwest. They provide an impressive musical backdrop for your listening pleasure, but when Big Mama Gayle is on stage, you'll find it impossible to look away.

Paul

Saxophone

Paul has been the lead saxophone player for Big Mama Gayle since his junior year of high school in 2012. A Forest Grove native and former member of the Willamette University Jazz Collective, Paul has been playing the sax since the age of 11. With musical influences such as Stevie Wonder, Tower of Power, and Earth, Wind & Fire, Paul's goal is not to make you listen to the music, but to make you feel the music in your soul.
